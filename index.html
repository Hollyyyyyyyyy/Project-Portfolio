About Project & Goals

Enhancing COVID-19 Literature Search with NLP Text Classification and Question Answering

Host organization: NCI(National computational infrastructure)
Supervisor: Jingbo wang

project focuses on text classification research funded by the National Computational Infrastructure (NCI). With the aim of increasing classification accuracy, the study utilizes Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques to analyze COVID-19 literature. By studying trends and patterns in this crucial area, researchers can gain insights into the challenges and impact of the pandemic. The project employs semi-supervised learning to reduce bias, improve accuracy, and identify key COVID-19 topics. By effectively categorizing vast amounts of COVID-19-related data, the research aims to inform public health policies and guide future research endeavors.

NLP (Multi-label classification):
Given the title and abstract of the publications, classify the label of the paper. The dataset is 550,000 publications related to covid19, with labels such as medicine, society, environment, etc.
Q & A (Question & Answer):
The user enters a query statement and the system searches all the publications in the database and returns the 3 most relevant recommended reads.

Progress

Project Progress and Milestones

Data preprocessing

Gadi Environment Setting

Week2

Gadi is the supercomputer of NCI. Interns have the permission to use Gadi and are allocated a usage quota. With Gadi, we can train models faster. A well-configured Gadi environment are built, which containing all the necessary NLP packages for text analysis and topic modelling.

Data Cleaning

Week3

NLP data cleaning involves text parsing, noise removal, lowercasing, and stopword removal. These steps refine data for improved quality and accuracy. By breaking down text, removing irrelevant elements, ensuring consistent word treatment, and eliminating common but uninformative words, NLP algorithms can extract meaningful insights from text data more effectively.

Data Labeling

Week4

In the data labelling process for semi-supervised learning, several aspects come into play. These include selecting an initial labeled dataset, designing strategies for active learning to identify additional data points for manual labeling, leveraging annotation tools or crowdsourcing for efficient labeling, ensuring label quality and consistency, and iteratively incorporating newly labeled data into the training process to enhance the model's performance and generalize better on unlabeled data.

Model training and evaluation

Multi-label classification

week5-week7

In my multi-label classification task, I employed the BERT model and performed fine-tuning to enhance its performance. Additionally, I leveraged data augmentation techniques to improve accuracy. Through these efforts, I achieved an impressive accuracy of 99.83%. Accurately categorizing published literature can help us better understand the trend of COVID-19 development and research hotspots.

Semi-supervised Learning

Week8-week9

I utilized labelled data and employed the self-training technique for predicting labels. Self-training involves using initially labelled data to train a model, and then using the model to predict labels for unlabeled data, which are then added as pseudo-labelled data for further training. This iterative process improves the model's performance by leveraging the additional information from the predicted labels.

Question and Answer

Week10-week11

I developed a question-answering model that enables users to input queries and retrieve the most relevant article. To determine relevance, I implemented cosine similarity as a criterion. By comparing the vector representations of user queries and article content, we calculated the cosine similarity scores. This allowed us to identify and present the article with the highest relevance to the user's query, facilitating effective information retrieval and knowledge exploration.

Achievement

Model and Results

In our project, we utilized the BERT model, a powerful language representation model, for text classification and question-answering tasks, leveraging its contextual understanding and advanced language modeling capabilities.
Our question-answering (Q&A) model consists of a recall stage and a ranking stage. In the recall stage, the model retrieves relevant passages. In the ranking stage, it ranks the passages to identify the most suitable answer for the user's query.
The result of our project demonstrates remarkable achievement, with our multilabel classification model achieving an accuracy of 99%. This high level of accuracy highlights the effectiveness of our approach in accurately classifying multiple labels in the dataset.
